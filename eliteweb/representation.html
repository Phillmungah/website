Introduction to Indian aviation industry 

Please consider some facts about India Aviation Industry illustrating the gigantic opportunity this market will present to business worldwide in future:
Since its inception Indian aviation industry has seen lot of ups & downs. The factor which matters is how well we as a nation have learned from our downs and been motivated by our ups. Coming back to 2012 we would like present some points which will help out all the willing hearts looking to work in those markets where every nook and corner provides you a new opportunity and a challenge to seize it. 

Current status - some important facts :

>> India has grown into the ninth largest aviation market in the world, with 29.8 million passengers travelling to and from the country. The number is expected to go up to 50 million passengers by 2015. The number of international flights to and from India had gone up to 706 per week.


>> Scheduled air services are available to and from 82 airport in India, as against only 50 in early 2000 & as many as 69 foreign airlines from 49 countries were flying into India at present. India ranks fourth after the United States, China and Japan in terms of domestic passenger's volume & expected number of domestic passengers will grow by 9-10 per cent per annum to reach a level of 150-180 million passengers by 2020.

>> In 2005, 2006 and 2007 especially, India saw growth in the high 20s and 30s (percentage points). In 2008, the oil price rise and subsequently the world recession impacted aviation worldwide and in India too. Indian Aviation Industry also have an open skies agreement with the U.S. -- a first of its kind. There is a lot more connectivity now between India and the U.S.

>> FDI is a fairly reasonable percentage. It is 49% at the moment. But in terms of aviation-related (areas) -- like airport infrastructure, greenfield airports - it is going to have 100%.
